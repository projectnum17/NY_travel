The High Line

The High Line is a unique park that arose on the site of an inactive elevated railway. Its length is almost two and a half kilometers, and it is located at a height of ten meters. Walking along this man-made miracle, you will feel as if you are in a serene oasis in the middle of the stone jungle from houses soaring into the sky.

One World Trade Center

Near the center you will see the national September 11 memorial, consisting of two fountains where the destroyed towers were. You can stand near the far corner of one of them and try to fit the fountain along with the blue skyscraper in the frame, or get closer to the glass giant, look up and take a picture.

The Statue of Liberty

50

The first thing that comes to mind from what you need to visit in New York is the Statue of Liberty on the island of the same name. The majestic goddess Libertas welcomes all adventurers arriving by water. It was she who was seen by millions of emigrants who came to America in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in search of a better life.

The Iron Building is located at the intersection of Broadway, Fifth Avenue and East 23rd Street. Although it looks more like a proud ocean liner than a huge iron, the name has already firmly stuck to it. This is one of the world's most famous venues for sports, music and entertainment events, which can accommodate up to 20,000 spectators.
